Pros and cons of 5G technology

2021-11-23


Advantages of 5G

Compared with 4G and all previous standards, the speed and latency improvements brought by 5G have obvious and significant advantages. Another advantage of 5G technology is the inherent improvement of device support, which benefits from the smaller size and more accurate directional signal transmission that will accompany 5G's MIMO antenna array.

The improved 5G network architecture will make the handover when users move from one cell to another more smoothly. This will improve the overall user experience by limiting data transmission interruptions and signal loss.

Defects and dangers of 5G technology

Like the inherent advantages of 5G, many obvious defects also stem from the shift to higher frequencies and the characteristics of radio signals in millimeter waves. Shorter coverage and more susceptibility to obstacles are the most obvious disadvantages of this type of technology.

In addition to obstacles such as buildings and trees, high frequencies are more susceptible to humidity and rainfall, so the originally limited range will be further challenged by suboptimal weather conditions. If more antennas can significantly improve the problem of limited coverage, then the aesthetic and environmental issues associated with this propagation will become another potential problem.

Other disadvantages of 5G technology are related to cost. The antenna array is only one aspect of the deployment cost. The maintenance, repair, and troubleshooting of these arrays will be proportional to the larger hardware capacity. Although millimeter wave antennas for devices have been developed, their complexity may make economies of scale ineffective in reducing prices, and their costs will eventually be passed on to consumers by increasing communication fees.

The concept of beams and smaller cell sizes in 5G decompose the basic coverage unit into packets much smaller than the old technology. These packages will be interactive and need to be configured and optimized to support coverage while maintaining capacity, while supporting mobility in appropriate scenarios.

Planning, deploying, managing, operating and optimizing a more flexible network architecture on a virtualized infrastructure will bring new challenges to operators and require new expertise.
