5G: Principles, Features

2021-11-20

Since the birth of the first generation of mobile network technology decades ago, this technology has undergone tremendous changes. Now, fifth-generation technology (5G) is expected to transmit data faster and more reliably than ever before.

What is 5G?

5G refers to the fifth-generation mobile network. It aims to expand existing 4G cellular networks or even replace them completely. Each generation of technology is determined by many factors, such as the technology used, the time interval (delay) between sending and receiving signals, and the speed at which data is transmitted over the network to connected devices. The 5G network is expected to achieve gigabit speeds. At the same time, 5G services greatly reduce latency and can extend coverage to remote areas.

However, 5G is currently only a blueprint, because only a few areas have established supporting infrastructure. South Korea has already promoted 5G technology nationwide, while Japan plans to complete network integration before hosting the next Olympic Games. The US Federal Communications Commission (FCC) and other countries or regions (such as Australia, China, and Europe) are also working with local service providers to expand 5G coverage.

What is the meaning of 5G?

With the increase of Internet users, 4G networks have become more and more congested. People use the same frequency network, which greatly increases the delay in daily use.

Over time, download speeds will become slower and slower, and overall performance will be affected. With the increasing popularity of mobile phones, the inconvenience this brings to consumers is obvious.

5G aims to solve these problems through the use of a variety of different technologies, and to increase download speeds by using low-frequency (below 6 GHz) signals.

The impact of 5G technology on consumers and businesses

The most obvious and direct impact on consumers will be the exponential increase in streaming media capacity. Traditional home Wi-Fi systems connected to the Internet via wires or cables may in some cases become 5G direct connections because the speed will be comparable to most fiber optic networks.

The long-term impact of 5G technology on the enterprise sector will be huge. Manufacturers of chips, basebands, and mobile phones are ready for revolutionary changes. Other industries, such as banking, automotive, and agriculture, will be significantly affected by the development of the Internet of Things (IoT). From ATMs to irrigation equipment, everything may eventually become part of a huge network of "smart" products.

The medical industry may be the most affected, with billions of wearable devices streaming data to doctors and clinicians and improving the transfer of large data files from one provider to another.
